2/11/2025- 

Shelton, Connecticut-based Hubbell Inc(HUB) is up ~11% over the last 52 weeks. Compared to the SP500 and SP500EW, the stock is up ~102% over the previous three years. The Electrical Equipment & Parts maker recently reported mixed results for the quarter ending on Dec24. In the press release, Mr. Gerben Bakker, Chairman, President & CEO, shared his confidence in the company’s ability to “deliver on our initial 2025 outlook,” citing “growth in net sales, operating margin, operating profit and free cash flow.”  The company expects to report 4-5% sales growth for the twelve months ending Dec25. Over the twelve months ending in Dec24, the company reported 5629M in sales(up 5% YoY), 1092M in Operating Income(up 5% YoY), an Operating Margin of 19%(unch YoY), and a Free Cash Flow of 811M(up 13% YoY) [Key Performance Indicators highlighted by Mr. Bakker in Q424 earning release]. The company recorded a Return on Investment(ROI), Return on Equity(ROE), and Return on Assets(ROA) of 23%, 24%, and 12%, respectively, compared to the trailing twelve quarter average of 22%, 23%, and 10%, respectively. Over the twelve quarters ending in Dec24, the company averaged a price-to-sales of 3.0x(±0.7). 

At 3.7x price-to-sales, shares should be around 405, considering the company successfully reported a 4-5% YoY sales growth [5854M to 5910M]. Rising animal spirits, i.e., price-to-sales of 4.5x, would suggest shares can trade ~490. The average price target on Yahoo Finance was 454 [383-507] (as of 2/11/2025). Reason to buy-  Despite a tariff-fueled zeitgeist and uncertainty around raw material procurement, the infrastructure spending promised by private firms to power their AI ambitions and the bipartisan infrastructure bill promising more than 73B to modernize the American Power infrastructure are positive catalysts for the narrative. Paired with the political climate that inclines and rewards companies to spend capital in Domestic/US-based projects, I believe Hubbell Inc(HUB) can benefit from such tailwinds considering around 90% and 85% of the company’s total Net Sales and Operating Income(FY21-FY23) is domestic, i.e., the United States.
